National Merit Scholarship Program

Award Amount: ₹50,000 | Awarded by: National Scholarship Foundation

Scholarship Overview

The National Merit Scholarship Program is an academic competition for recognition and scholarships that began in 1955. High school students enter the National Merit Program by taking the Preliminary SAT/National Merit Scholarship Qualifying Test (PSAT/NMSQT®)—which serves as an initial screen of approximately 1.5 million entrants each year—and by meeting published program entry and participation requirements.

Eligibility Criteria

  • check_circle

    Must be enrolled full-time as a high school student, progressing normally toward graduation.

  • check_circle

    Must be a citizen of India or a permanent resident.

  • check_circle

    Must have a minimum GPA of 3.5 on a 4.0 scale.

  • check_circle

    Must plan to enroll full-time in an accredited college or university in the academic year.

Application Process

1

Register and Qualify

Take the PSAT/NMSQT® in the specified year of the program and meet the eligibility criteria.

2

Submit Application

Semifinalists must complete the scholarship application, which includes an essay and information about extracurricular activities.

3

Finalist Notification

Await notification of advancement to Finalist standing, then scholarship winners are selected from the Finalist group.
